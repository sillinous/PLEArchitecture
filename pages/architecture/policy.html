Policy Framework

Concrete mechanisms for implementing the post-labor transition

From Vision to Implementation

The Pyramids aren't just theory—they're implementation guides. Each layer has specific policy mechanisms that can be advocated for, piloted, and scaled.

Pyramid of Prosperity

Income distribution policies

1

Universal Basic Services

Healthcare, education, housing guarantees, public transit expansion

2

Sovereign Wealth Funds

National investment funds with citizen dividends, data trusts, carbon pricing revenue

3

Cooperative Incentives

ESOP tax advantages, DAO legal frameworks, community land trust support

4

Capital Access Programs

Baby bonds, universal capital grants, investment account matching

5

Labor Protections

High minimum wages, portable benefits, strong worker rights

Pyramid of Power

Democratic leverage policies

1

Digital Identity Standards

Self-sovereign identity systems, blockchain property registries

2

Open Payment Infrastructure

Public instant payment systems (like India's UPI), interoperability mandates

3

Transparency Mandates

Open procurement requirements, algorithmic accountability, beneficial ownership

4

Participatory Democracy

Participatory budgeting pilots, citizens assemblies, digital town halls

5

Governance Innovation

Constitutional review processes, sunset clause requirements, policy experimentation

Implementation Timeline

A phased approach to building the post-labor infrastructure

Now - 2027

Foundation Phase

Build coalition, develop detailed policy papers, expand community, engage early-adopter policymakers

2027 - 2030

Pilot Phase

Municipal and state-level pilots, sovereign wealth fund proposals, participatory budgeting expansion

2030 - 2035

Scaling Phase

National-level policy adoption, international coordination, multiple Pyramid layers operational

2035+

Transition Phase

Capital ownership broadly distributed, labor no longer primary income source, post-labor economy operational
