Latent Value Network

A framework for mapping, understanding, and surfacing the vast substrate of human value that markets have never learned to measure ‚Äî and that becomes foundational in a post-labor economy.

GDP counts the nurse who provides care but not the parent who does the same. It counts the therapist but not the friend. It prices the consultant but not the volunteer who solves the same problem. The invisible substrate of human activity ‚Äî care, community, creativity, knowledge transfer, social cohesion ‚Äî is not peripheral to the economy. It is foundational. And in a post-labor world, it becomes everything.

‚Äî Core Argument, Latent Value Network

‚ö° The Problem

The formal economy captures only a fraction of the value humans create. The rest ‚Äî the care, mentorship, community organizing, ecological stewardship, knowledge sharing, mutual aid, and creative contribution ‚Äî remains latent: present, real, and essential, but unmeasured, uncompensated, and therefore vulnerable.

This invisibility is not accidental. It reflects systematic market failures (public goods, externalities), measurement failures (GDP was designed to count transactions, not welfare), power asymmetries (care work has historically been performed by those with least market leverage), and cultural blind spots (laborism treats only paid work as real work).

In a labor-based economy, this invisibility is painful but survivable ‚Äî formal wages provide a separate income stream. In a post-labor economy, when automation eliminates wage income, the question of how we recognize and sustain latent value becomes existential. We cannot build prosperity beyond work if we cannot see the work that has always been beyond markets.

üí¨ Discussion: Open Questions

The LVN concept risks being everything and therefore nothing. Rigorous scoping is essential. These are the questions that most need community input:

How should "latent" be defined ‚Äî unmeasured, uncompensated, or unrecognized?
These are meaningfully different. Open source work is measurable but often uncompensated. Ecological value may be both ‚Äî but for different reasons.
What distinguishes LVN from existing frameworks ‚Äî Putnam's social capital, Folbre's care economy, Ostrom's commons?
The LVN synthesizes these traditions, but needs to articulate what's new. Otherwise it's just relabeling.
How do we prevent "latent value recognition" from becoming surveillance capitalism?
Any mechanism to measure and compensate latent value risks requiring surveillance of intimate human activities. What are the hard limits?
Is the relationship between UBI and LVN bidirectional?
UBI may liberate people to contribute to LVN (no survival pressure). But does LVN contribution become a moral expectation that undermines the unconditional nature of UBI?
Can ecological value be part of LVN, or is it a separate category requiring different mechanisms?
Ecosystem services are latent in the economic sense, but they're produced by nature, not human networks. Does the "network" framing hold?
What does a "Value Commons" look like as an ownership model for latent value?
Neither public property (state-owned) nor private (individually-owned). Something like a commons governed by contributors. How would this be structured?
What is the most important thing this framework needs to get right?
Open synthesis question. All perspectives welcome.

üî¨ Research Threads

Literature Review: Care Economy, Social Capital, Commons, Gift Economy
Map the existing academic landscape. Key authors: Folbre, Putnam, Ostrom, Mauss, Illich. Identify what LVN adds vs. reframes.
Map Existing Partial Solutions
Wikipedia, open source, timebanks, mutual aid networks, community land trusts. What mechanisms worked? What failed and why?
Develop Rigorous Latent Value Taxonomy
Define and classify categories. Establish criteria for inclusion. Distinguish from adjacent concepts.
Model UBI ‚Üí LVN Contribution ‚Üí Collective Prosperity
Build a conceptual (and eventually quantitative) model of how basic income enables latent value contribution, which feeds back into collective wealth.
Design Pilot Measurement Methodology
How would a community actually measure its latent value? What indicators? What data sources? Privacy constraints?
Integrate with PRIME Framework
Add LVN as an evaluation dimension to the PRIME policy assessment tool. What does it look like to score a proposal on latent value impact?
